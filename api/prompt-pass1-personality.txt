# DETAILED ANALYSIS GUIDELINES

## MINDSET ANALYSIS (Q17-36)

### Calculation Method
1. **Romantic Score** = Average of Q17, Q18, Q19, Q20, Q21, Q29, Q30, Q34
   - These questions measure belief in soul mates, magical connection, effortless love
   - High scores (4-5) indicate idealistic, emotion-driven approach

2. **Resolute Score** = Average of Q22, Q23, Q24, Q25, Q26, Q27, Q28, Q31, Q32, Q33, Q35
   - These questions measure commitment focus, dedication, willingness to work
   - High scores (4-5) indicate pragmatic, covenant-driven approach

3. **Classification**:
   - If Romantic > Resolute + 0.5: "Romantic Mindset"
   - If Resolute > Romantic + 0.5: "Resolute Mindset"
   - Otherwise: "Balanced Mindset"

### Description Guidelines

**For Romantic Mindset** (write 2-3 paragraphs including):
- Opening: "You approach marriage with more idealism than most couples."
- Cite specific beliefs from Q17-21 responses
- Include: "In fact, you are nearly twice as likely as the average person to believe [specific belief from their high-scoring questions]."
- Explain what "soul mate" means to them based on Q29-30
- Discuss expectations for effortless harmony
- WARNING: "Love conquers all for you, but research shows marriages require intentional effort. Your idealism is beautiful but needs to be balanced with realistic expectations for conflicts and hard seasons."

**For Resolute Mindset** (write 2-3 paragraphs including):
- Opening: "You are a true believer in marriage as a lifelong covenant."
- Cite Q22-25 scores on commitment and dedication
- Include: "You have the highest marriage motivation of any mindset category. You are more than twice as likely as your peers to say: 'Divorce is not an option.'"
- Discuss their view of marriage as work (Q31-33)
- If Q26 is high: "You likely want children and see marriage as creating family."
- STRENGTH: "Your dedication will carry you through difficult times. However, be careful not to neglect the emotional, romantic aspects that keep marriages vibrant."

**For Balanced Mindset** (write 2-3 paragraphs including):
- Opening: "You hold a balanced view of marriage, blending idealism with realism."
- Explain which aspects lean romantic (specific Q#s) and which lean resolute (specific Q#s)
- This is actually ideal: "Research shows the best outcomes come from couples who embrace both the beauty AND the work of marriage."

### How Mindsets Mesh

Write 200-250 words analyzing:
1. **If Same Mindset**: Discuss shared values but also potential blind spots (e.g., two romantics may avoid necessary conflicts)
2. **If Different Mindsets**: Discuss how to bridge the gap
   - Romantic + Resolute: "The Romantic brings passion and vision; the Resolute brings stability and persistence. The key is honest discussions about expectations—especially during hard times when the Romantic may question the relationship while the Resolute digs in deeper."
3. **Specific Recommendations**: Include 1-2 actionable suggestions

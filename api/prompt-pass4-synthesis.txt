### Purpose

- Personality dynamics (Cooperating/Affirming/Directing/Analyzing)
- Communication styles
- Conflict approaches
- Expectations for marriage

### Structure

**Paragraph 1: Name the Combination and Initial Assessment** (80-100 words)

"You don't share the same mindset, but you both share a passion for a loving marriage. {person1_name}'s {mindset_type} combined with {dynamics_type} personality creates someone who [specific behavioral tendency]. {person2_name}'s {mindset_type} combined with {dynamics_type} personality creates someone who [contrasting or complementary tendency]."

Then assess compatibility:
- **Same Mindset**: "This shared worldview is a strength, though you'll need to watch for blind spots. Two Romantics may avoid necessary conflicts; two Resolutes may neglect romance."

Draw from multiple data sources to predict challenges:

# SYNTHESIS PRINCIPLES

## 1. Cross-Reference Across Passes

Never analyze a dimension in isolation. Always connect:
- "Your Romantic Mindset (Pass 1) combined with limited social support (Pass 2, score: {score}) means you may lean too heavily on each other for all emotional needs, creating unhealthy dependence."
- "Your Affirming Spouse communication style (Pass 3) amplifies your High resilience (Pass 1, score: {score}), making you a consistently optimistic partner who lifts {partner_name}'s spirits."

## 2. Use Specific Data Points

Always cite actual scores, levels, and assessments:
- "With wellbeing scores of 88 and 97..."
- "Having dated for 18-24 months..."
- "Your social support averaging 72 out of 100..."
- "Given your Moderate self-concept (score: 6.5) and {partner}'s Strong self-concept (score: 9.2)..."

## 3. Predict Real-Life Scenarios

Move beyond descriptions to predictions:
- "When you face your first major financial stress, here's what will likely happen: {person1_name} will want to talk it through immediately (Affirming Spouse communication), while {person2_name} will withdraw to analyze options (Analyzing Spouse tendency). {person1_name} will interpret the withdrawal as rejection, creating secondary conflict."

## 4. Balance Strengths and Challenges

For every challenge identified, note a corresponding strength:
- "While your different mindsets could cause friction around expectations, your shared Strong wellbeing scores (88 and 97) give you the emotional health to navigate these differences productively."

## 5. Provide Chapter References

When recommending focus areas, cite specific chapters:
- "Pay particular attention to Chapter 6 of your SYMBIS book: 'Do You Know How to Fight a Good Fight?' Your hot topic rankings show Money as #1 for both of you, and your different conflict styles will need intentional management."
